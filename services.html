Math

  • Algebra I

    • Algebra is a broad category and is essentially using math in combination with unknowns. It is a large and foundational subject that typically spans multiple years, with a few years of pre-algebra in middleschool.

    In Algebra I you should already be comfortable with equations where you'll be expected to solve for \(x\) like this: \[\frac{x-2}{3} = 7\]

    You need to have a firm understanding of the order of operations (\(\text{PEMDAS}\)), be confident in the all of the properties of multiplication, division, addition, and subtraction, and understand that equations allow us to substitute one value for another: \[ \begin{align} \text{Let }\; \; b = 7\\ \\ \text{If }\; \; 3x + 1b &= 2\\ \\ 3x + 1(7) &= 2\\ 3x + 7 &=2\\ 3x &= -5\\ x &= \frac{-5}{3} \end{align} \]

    In Algebra I, a broad range of topics are introduced as a way to deepen your algebra and arithmetic skills while also broadening your mathematical horizons. The deepest studied will lines and their equations:

    \[y = \frac{x}{2} + 2\]

    Students in Algebra I will be expected to master many different aspects of working with lines, including: finding the slope, regression, finding perpendicular and parallel lines, and applications of lines. By the end of Algebra I, students will come to understand lines as more than simple equations; they are linear functions. The distinction between these terms should be meaningful as it's the bedrock for Algebra II.

    Aside from lines, students should be introduced to many other topics. The most common being:

    • inequalities
    • exponents
      \( \begin{align} y^4 &= y \cdot y \cdot y \cdot y \\ y^4 \cdot y^3 &= y^{4+3}\\ \frac{y^4}{y^2} &= y^4-2\\ y^{-1} &= \frac{1}{y}\\ y^0 &= 1\\ \end{align} \)
    • radicals
      \( \begin{align} \sqrt{x^2} &=x \\ \sqrt[n]{x^n} &=x \\ \sqrt{x} &= x^{\frac{1}{2}} \\ (\sqrt[3]{x})^2 &= x^{\frac{2}{3}} \\ \sqrt{72} = \sqrt{9\cdot 4 \cdot 2} &= 6 \sqrt{2} \end{align} \)
    • distribution
    • factoring
      \( \begin{align} 3x^2-\frac{x}{2} &=x(3x-\frac{1}{2})\\ x^2 + 3x -10 &= (x+5)(x-2)\\ 2x^2 + 7x + 3 &= (2x+1)(x+3) \end{align} \)
    • quadratic functions
